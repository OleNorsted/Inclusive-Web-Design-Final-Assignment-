Inspired by the UN’s 13th sustainable development goal.

The Challenge

“appnavn” faces a variety of problems and concerns in regards to climate change:

  1. 1. Sustainable agriculture, forestry, and water use.
  2. 2. Being climate-friendly requires a lifestyle change.
  3. 3. Lack of information and guidance.
  4. 4. Contribution is not visible.
  5. 5. A bad reputation among ordinary consumers.

Tackling the climate problem is not easy. The problem becomes more apparent year after year, as we see more extreme weather, melting of the poles and deserts becoming larger. These alarming consequences of climate change can traced back to human behavior, and a lack of unsustainable habits as simple as eating. “source2 https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/releases/amp-66-4-251.pdf ”. All of us are encouraged to reduce our ecological footprint on the planet. We pressure politicians into making changes for the better, donate to various climate change causes and most countries have become incredibly good at recycling. What most if not all of us have in common though, is that we like to eat. Most people do not know that methane has a global warming potential, 86 times greater than that of CO2 on a year time frame. And what makes up for much of the planet's methane production? Agriculture. “Source 3: https://www.cowspiracy.com/facts “

The Social Problem

The climate problem is not new. We see it everywhere in media and society. “The end of civilization”-articles and the like are impossible to ignore, and you should not ignore it either. Yet, we are not only facing climate change itself. The average person does not want, and or does not have time and resources to dedicate themselves towards climate change. They are also way less likely to change their way of eating for it. The main excuses for not changing to a more eco friendly lifestyle is because it takes too much prepping. Other reasons are that the variety of recipes are low and not diverse enough. The root cause of this misinformation between consumers, are often acts extreme veganism that often distracts from the benefits of it. External forces (media, roles in society, social networks) are very important aspects of society that often hinders ordinary consumers to take the conscious step towards a better tomorrow. So when the main part of society seems focused on difficult and far-fetched solutions like hydrogen-cars, complex options of travel and expensive solar-panel plans, the average consumer is at a loss for options and tasks that can help turn the tide of climate change. That is where “appnavn” comes in. The apps main goal is information. We see that info about non-meat options are scarce and difficult to find. Thats why our ambition is to give people the option to share their info, catalogue their progress. This will, inspire others to be creative towards the subtle art of food.

The solution

“appnavn” will tackle these climate change problems by: 1. Making sustainable agriculture, forestry and water-use possible for the average joe. 2. Providing an easy and smooth transition towards a more eco-friendly and healthier lifestyle. 3. Providing plenty of info and guidance in the form of sharing recipes and articles that relates to good, non-meat food options. 4. Tracing and making the resources you have saved over time visible. 5. Helping you and others realise that a non-meat lifestyle is healthier and not as scary as some people think. Eco-friendliness made easy. “appnavn” introduces you to a world of new tastes and wonders and connects you to other like-minded individuals near and far. Prepare meals far in advance with our meal-planner. Expand your cooking skills with our search and share function, where you can see recipes others have made, including hand-picked recipes by our staff from throughout the internet. Our ambition here at “appnavn” is to pave the way for users to involve themselves in the fight against climate change in a fun and healthy way. We tackle the climate challenge by making it easy for everyone to change to a non-meat lifestyle, with custom settings for each user that can be changed at any time. Your carbon footprint will be noticeable, as we track every meal you add with our app to your over-all contribution towards the climate. We do this by implementing a calculator that adds how much resources and lives you have saved through each green meal from the first recipe that you add to the list. ”Link source4: https://www.wri.org/blog/2016/04/sustainable-diets-what-you-need-know-12-charts” “Link source 5: http://static.ewg.org/reports/2011/meateaters/pdf/methodology_ewg_meat_eaters_guide_to_health_and_climate_2011.pdf “ Everything will be available for you in the app. How many meals you want to add, and what kind of meal-plan, from pescatarian to vegan, is up to you to decide. Together we can turn the tide and fight climate change, one meal at a time.
